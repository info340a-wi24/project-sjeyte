Harmony - Mental Wellness Platform

Problem/Data Domain

Many people struggle with mental health issues like anxiety and depression, especially during difficult times. The pandemic has exacerbated these issues, emphasizing the need for accessible mental health care. Minority groups often face additional barriers to accessing mental health support, such as stigma, lack of culturally competent care, and socioeconomic disparities.

Existing Attempts: Several mental health apps exist, offering services ranging from mood tracking to guided meditation. However, gaps in accessibility and personalization remain, particularly for underserved communities.

References: WHO: Mental Health Strengthening Our Response, National Institute of Mental Health: Topics, Mental and Behavioral Health - African Americans

Web App Description

Harmony is a mental wellness platform tailored for anyone seeking support, with a focus on accessibility. My aim is to provide a space where users can easily find and engage with mental health resources, fostering a community that supports each individual's journey towards better mental health.

Upon signing up, users will complete an initial survey or questionnaire that assesses their mental health based on their responses. This survey will help provide a preliminary diagnosis and guide users towards relevant resources and support within the app.

Users engage with Harmony through features like the Mood Tracker, for logging emotional states; Guided Meditation, for relaxation; Resource Directory, for educational content; and Personalized Recommendations, which suggest activities based on user preferences, demographics, and the results of their initial assessment. These tools are designed to be interactive, allowing users to actively participate in their mental wellness journey by providing inputs, making selections, and receiving tailored content.

Harmony addresses the need for accessible mental health support by offering a platform where users can discover culturally relevant resources, connect with others who share similar experiences, and receive personalized care based on their individual needs. This approach empowers users to take steps towards improving their mental health in a supportive, engaging environment.

Proposed Application

My web application, Harmony, aims to bridge the accessibility gap in mental health support by providing a comprehensive suite of tools and resources tailored to individual needs.

Users:

My platform is designed for individuals seeking to improve their mental wellness through self-guided tools and resources, with a particular focus on serving minority communities.

Interactive Features:

  • Initial Survey: Users complete a questionnaire to assess their mental health and receive a preliminary diagnosis.
  • Mood Tracker: Users can log their daily emotions and view trends over time.
  • Guided Meditation: Users can access a library of guided meditations for relaxation and stress relief.
  • Resource Directory: Users can browse a curated collection of articles, videos, and podcasts on mental health topics, filtered by cultural relevance and user preferences.
  • Personalized Recommendations: Based on user input, demographics, initial assessment results, and engagement history, the app will suggest tailored resources and activities to support their mental wellness journey.

Data Source: User-generated data and reputable mental health resources, such as the Office of Minority Health, will inform my content and recommendations.

Impact: By offering accessible, personalized mental health support, Harmony aims to make a significant difference in the lives of those struggling with mental health challenges, particularly in underserved communities.

Considerations and Challenges

  • Designing an intuitive, user-friendly interface that showcases my skills in creating accessible web applications. I will conduct user testing within the scope of the project to refine the app's usability.
  • Curating a diverse, culturally relevant resource library that demonstrates my ability to gather and present information effectively. I will use publicly available resources and research to ensure the quality and appropriateness of my content.
